# Analysis 4-1: agent taxonomy and formal model space

## Main question

What is the minimal but theoretically meaningful space of candidate agent types that should be compared in the next stage of the project?

More specifically:

> Beyond the existing \( \rho \)-attenuation family, what additional forms of structural approximation or heuristic approximation should be considered as candidate models of bounded human inference on graph-structured tasks?

## Why this analysis is needed

The current modelling framework is centred on a single family of approximate agents that perform inference on the true graph while attenuating the effective influence of more distant factors.

This is useful, but potentially too narrow.

Several alternative possibilities have now become salient:

1. participants may internally **rewrite the graph** into a simpler structure before inference;
2. participants may represent **local clusters or cliques** rather than individual nodes and edges;
3. some apparently new heuristics may in fact be mathematically equivalent to the current \( \rho = 0 \) local model;
4. variation currently attributed to \( \rho \) may partly reflect unmodelled variation in factor strength \( \beta \).

Before running further simulations or designing new experimental conditions, we therefore need a clear formal taxonomy of candidate agent families.

## Primary goals

### Goal 1. Define a unified agent taxonomy

Construct a principled taxonomy of candidate bounded-inference agents, partitioned into conceptually distinct classes.

At minimum, the taxonomy should distinguish between:

- **fixed-graph inference families**, where the true graph structure is preserved but some aspect of inference is attenuated or simplified;
- **structure-rewrite families**, where the internal graph is transformed prior to inference;
- **heuristic summary families**, where outputs are generated by local aggregation rules that may not preserve full probabilistic semantics.

### Goal 2. Formalise a minimal candidate set

Provide explicit mathematical definitions for a minimal candidate family set that is rich enough to support the next round of discrimination analyses, but small enough to remain tractable.

The initial target set should include:

- \( M_{\mathrm{exact}} \)
- \( M_0 \)
- \( M_{\rho} \)
- \( M_{\rho,\beta} \) or equivalent \( \beta \)-augmented family
- \( M_{\mathrm{star}} \)
- \( M_{\mathrm{cluster}} \)

### Goal 3. Clarify theoretical distinctness

For each candidate family, explain:

- what structure is preserved;
- what structure is approximated or discarded;
- what its free parameters are;
- how it differs from neighbouring families;
- whether it is psychologically plausible as a model of human bounded inference.

### Goal 4. Identify reducibility and redundancy

Determine whether any proposed heuristic is actually reducible to an existing family.

In particular, assess whether the proposed local "sum log evidence from neighbours, then transform to output" strategy is formally equivalent to \( M_0 \), unless enriched by an additional nonlinear response transformation.

## Candidate families to formalise

## 1. Exact inference

### Family
\( M_{\mathrm{exact}} \)

### Informal description
Inference is performed exactly on the true graph with the true factors.

### Role in the taxonomy
Normative benchmark.

---

## 2. Strictly local / nearest-neighbour inference

### Family
\( M_0 \)

### Informal description
Only direct neighbours of the target contribute to the target belief. No multi-step propagation is represented.

### Role in the taxonomy
Baseline bounded agent. Also the likely formal equivalent of several local-evidence heuristics.

---

## 3. Fixed-graph attenuation family

### Family
\( M_{\rho} \)

### Informal description
Inference is carried out on the true graph, but the effective influence of factors decays as a function of graph distance or elimination depth according to parameter \( \rho \).

### Role in the taxonomy
Current core approximation family.

---

## 4. Fixed-graph attenuation plus variable factor strength

### Family
\( M_{\rho,\beta} \) or a similar two-parameter family

### Informal description
As above, but with explicit freedom in edge/factor coupling strength.

### Role in the taxonomy
Tests whether effects currently attributed to structural attenuation may be confounded with or explained by factor-strength variation.

---

## 5. Target-centred star approximation

### Family
\( M_{\mathrm{star}} \)

### Informal description
The agent internally rewrites the graph into a target-centred star. All other observed or latent nodes influence the target through direct effective pairwise potentials, with strength and possibly sign depending on shell distance or node class.

### Key theoretical motivation
This captures the idea that the participant may collapse all distal structure into direct target-centred influences rather than preserving relay structure.

### Important modelling question
Should the effective pairwise parameters depend only on shell parity, shell index, or rooted role class?

---

## 6. Local cluster / clique / junction-lite approximation

### Family
\( M_{\mathrm{cluster}} \)

### Informal description
Instead of representing only nodes and edges, the agent groups some local substructures—such as neighbour cliques, local triangles, or short-range induced subgraphs—into a small cluster representation and performs approximate inference over this compressed local structure.

### Key theoretical motivation
Participants may chunk local structure into "blocks" rather than approximate all nonlocal information by pairwise links.

### Important modelling constraint
The family should be defined in a lightweight and implementable way; a full generic junction-tree model is not required at this stage.

---

## Optional future family: elimination-order approximation

### Possible family
\( M_{\mathrm{elim}} \), \( M_{\mathrm{greedy\mbox{-}elim}} \), or similar

### Informal description
Inference is approximated by a limited or heuristic variable elimination schedule.

### Recommendation for this analysis
Discuss as a possible later extension, but do not include in the core minimal family set unless a simple and clearly distinct formalisation can be given.

## Questions this analysis should answer

1. What are the major conceptually distinct classes of approximation relevant to this project?
2. Which candidate families are genuinely distinct rather than reparameterisations of one another?
3. What is the smallest candidate family set worth carrying forward into simulation-based separability analyses?
4. Which families have clear psychological interpretations, and which are merely algorithmic possibilities?
5. Where should \( \beta \) live in the model space: fixed constant, nuisance parameter, or core family parameter?

## Deliverables

### D1. Taxonomy document
A short written document defining the main classes of bounded-inference agent.

### D2. Unified notation table
For each family, specify:
- internal graph representation;
- factor representation;
- free parameters;
- output belief object;
- relation to exact inference.

### D3. Family comparison table
A compact side-by-side table summarising:
- graph preserved or rewritten;
- local versus nonlocal sensitivity;
- expected sensitivity to rooted dimensions D1, D2, D3;
- expected computational simplicity;
- psychological interpretability.

### D4. Redundancy assessment
A brief argument stating whether the proposed local log-evidence heuristic is:
- exactly equivalent to \( M_0 \),
- approximately equivalent to \( M_0 \),
- or a distinct family only after adding a separate response transform.

### D5. Visual schematic
A conceptual figure showing the difference between:
- exact graph inference,
- attenuation on the true graph,
- target-centred star rewrite,
- local cluster/junction-lite rewrite.

## Success criteria

This analysis is successful if it delivers all of the following:

1. a minimal family set of at least four and at most six candidate agents;
2. formal definitions precise enough to support simulation in later analyses;
3. a clear statement of which proposed heuristics are redundant with existing families;
4. an explicit recommendation of which families should be taken forward into Analysis 4-2;
5. a coherent conceptual distinction between parameter approximation and structure approximation.

## Out of scope

This analysis should **not** yet:

- run large-scale separability simulations;
- fit models to participant data;
- optimise new stimuli;
- develop a full generic junction-tree implementation;
- introduce a large number of loosely motivated agent variants.

## Recommended decisions if ambiguity remains

If there is uncertainty about whether to include many families, prefer the following minimal working set for downstream analysis:

- \( M_{\mathrm{exact}} \)
- \( M_0 \)
- \( M_{\rho} \)
- \( M_{\rho,\beta} \)
- \( M_{\mathrm{star}} \)
- \( M_{\mathrm{cluster}} \)

If \( M_{\mathrm{cluster}} \) cannot be made operational cleanly, replace it temporarily with a simpler local-structure surrogate, but do not replace it with a family that is only a weak variant of \( M_{\rho} \).

## Relationship to Analysis 4-2

Analysis 4-2 will take the family set defined here and test whether these families are behaviourally distinguishable on the current rooted stimulus library using KL-based diagnostics and confusion analyses.

Therefore, the output of this analysis must be simulation-ready.
